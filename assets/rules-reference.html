Battlechess Armada Rules

Battlechess Armada

Naval Strategy Meets Chess Tactics

1. Objective

Primary: Sink the enemy ♔ King Flagship.

Secondary (optional): Preagreed alternate win conditions (e.g., eliminate both King and Queen).

2. Components

Board:

12×12 grid (A–L, 1–12).

Two boards, separated by blinder (game box serves as blinder).

Fleet (16 pieces per player):

♔ King Flagship – 1

♕ Queen Dreadnought – 1

♖ Rook Destroyer – 2

♗ Bishop Frigate – 2

♘ Knight Submarine – 2

♙ Pawn Corvette – 8

Markers:

Hit/Damage (red) – 30 per player

Miss (white) – 30 per player

Enemy location (red pawns) – 20 per player

Piece ID markers (silhouettes) – 16 per player

Pass Phase coin – 1 per player (Steady Shot / Repair Crew)

Damage counters (1–6 dice)

Shifting Currents Tokens (blue) - 2 (1 per player)

3. Deployment Zone, Setup and Start of Game

Deploy fleet in first 3 rows closest to you.

Pawns in row 2 or 3.

Named pieces in rows 1–2.

Bishops on opposite colors.

Place Shifting Currents zones.

Flip Pass Phase coin to decide who goes first.

4. Turn Structure

Each turn has 3 phases:

Movement Phase (or Command Orders)

Targeting/Action/Passing Phase (includes Recon, Mine deployment, Pass abilities)

Combat Resolution Phase

5. Core Rules

Movement:

One piece moves per turn.

Announce destination aloud but not piece type.

Opponent places generic pawn marker on their side of the board in the destination.

If destination occupied → Ramming Mechanic occurs (both ships take 1 damage, move back one square). See Mechanics (Expanded) for additional details.

Pawns promote to Queen upon reaching far rank.

Targeting:

Only the moved piece may target.

Announce target square within range.

Base damage = 1 (modified by cards or abilities).

Alternatives: Recon, Mine deployment, or Pass Phase ability. See Mechanics (Expanded) for additional details.

Combat Resolution:

Apply damage (base + modifiers).

Adjust HP.

Destroyed ships removed.

6. Mechanics (Expanded)

Ramming

If a ship moves into a square occupied by an enemy, both ships take 1 damage, and each player announces which ship was rammed. The moving ship then steps back one square in its original direction. If the new square is also occupied by an enemy, repeat the process.

Any ship identified through ramming or by a special action card must announce its movement along with the usual destination announcement.

Passing Target Phase Abilities:

Steady Shot: Pass Targeting Phase (Turn 3+). Next activation, add +1 damage to base attack. Must be used immediately next turn or lost.

Repair Crew at the Ready: Pass Targeting Phase (Turn 3+). Coin flip: flames = repair 1 damage, crosshair = no effect. Applies if ship was damaged during opponent’s last attack OR already had damage. Pawn Corvettes cannot benefit.

Resupply:

Skip entire turn (no movement, targeting, or combat resolution).

Draw 1 Special Action card.

Cannot be used until Turn 3.

Shifting Currents:

Place 2 Current tokens (miss markers), 1 per player, covering 3×3 zones.

Roll die when any piece entering zone: 1–3 → drift backward; 4–6 → drift forward announce new location.

If drifted into occupied square → Ramming.

Attacks from within zone → 50% chance of drift (roll a die)

1-3 = destination square is fired upon.

4-6 = missed shot

After entry, roll die: 1-3 = zone shifts 3 squares randomly, 4-6 = remains in place.

Command Orders (King Flagship only):

Instead of moving/attacking, King issues one order to ally within 3 spaces of the King:

Full Speed Ahead → +1 movement range this turn.

Concentrated Fire → +1 damage if attacking this turn.

Defensive Maneuver → reduce incoming damage by 1 until next turn.

Cannot stack with Special Action cards or Pass abilities.

Consumes King’s action (he cannot move or attack that turn).

Reconnaissance:

Instead of targeting, ship performs Recon.

Player announces a square within range.

Opponent reveals: Whether square is occupied or mined (identity hidden).

Mine Field:

Mine tokens (grey markers).

Instead of targeting, place a mine directly behind from the direction that the piece moved.

Mines remain hidden until triggered.

Enemy enters → mine detonates, 2 damage, token removed.

Friendly enters → mine revealed, no detonation.

Opponent targets mined square → mine detonates, removed.

Only 3 mines active at a time per player.

Only one mine per square.

7. Special Action Cards

30-card deck.

Draw 5 at start (plus via Resupply).

One-time use.

Must be played before phase ends.

Cards may override normal movement/targeting rules.
